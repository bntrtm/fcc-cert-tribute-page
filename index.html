Betty Marion White

A pioneer in American Television

Here's a time line of Betty White's life:

  • 1922 - Born in Oak Park, Illinois
  • 1949 - Begins looking for work in movie studios. After being turned down for being "unphotogenic", she turns to radio.
  • 1952 - Begins hosting and producing The Betty White Show. With this show, she was the first woman to produce a national TV show as well as the first producer to hire a female director.
  • 1959 - Makes her professional stage debut in a week-long production of the play Third Best Sport.
  • 1961 - Having become a staple of many talk shows and television game shows, she begins to make appearances on the hit game show Password over a span of fifteen years. She would go on to make many more appearances on similar game shows throughout her career.
  • 1977 - Stars in her own television sitcom named The Betty White Show. The show received poor ratings and was canceled after one season due to being placed in the same timeslot as Monday Night Football.
  • 1983 - Becomes the first woman to be awarded a Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Game Show Host.
  • 1985 - Makes her first appearance as Rose Nylund on the NBC sitcom The Golden Girls. The show would become a defining time in her career, as she was nominated for the Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series Emmy Award for each year of the show's seven-season run.
  • 1988 - Receives an Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series award at the Emmy Awards show for her work on The Golden Girls.
  • 1992 - The Golden Girls comes to an end, but she would later reprise the role in its one-season spinoff, The Golden Palace, as well as in guest appearances on other NBC TV shows.
  • 1996 - Receives an Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series award, her second in her career, at the Emmy Awards show for her single appearance as herself on The John Larroquette Show.
  • 2009 - Stars in the romantic comedy film The Proposal alongside co-stars Sandra Bullock and Ryan Reynolds. In the same year, she appeared in an iconic Snicker's advertisement for Mars, Incorporated made to air during the 2010 Super Bowl XLIV as part of a new advertising campaign.
  • 2010 - Hosts Saturday Night Live after a grassroots campaign on Facebook emerged which aimed to get her to do so. She was awarded a 2010 Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series for the appearance on the popular sketch and variety show.
  • 2012 - wins a Grammy Award for Best Spoken Word Recording for her narration of her bestselling 2011 book If You Ask Me (And of Course You Won't).
  • 2015 - makes a second appearance on SNL for its 40th anniversary special.
  • 2021 - dies at the age of 99, just seventeen days before her 100th birthday.
